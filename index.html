Root Causes Analysis

1. Overwhelmed Recruiters

Recruiter workloads increased 26% year-over-year in Q3 2024. With hundreds of applications per role, individual communication becomes logistically impossible without automated systems.

"Delivering bad news is emotionally taxing, so many opt for silence as the path of least resistance."

2. Ghost Jobs Epidemic

45% of HR professionals admit their companies post jobs with no immediate intent to hire‚Äîused to gauge market interest or build resume pipelines. This guarantees mass ghosting from the start.

3. Broken Technology

Legacy Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) fail to parse resumes correctly and lack automated rejection workflows. When systems don't trigger closure emails, ghosting becomes the passive default.

4. No Accountability or Training

Few organizations provide formal training on candidate communication or measure hiring managers on candidate experience. Without standards or consequences, unprofessional behavior becomes normalized.
