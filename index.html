Executive Summary

Employer ghosting has evolved from an occasional professional discourtesy into a systemic breakdown in the hiring process. Our analysis of over 1 million data points reveals that the practice has more than doubled since 2020, with 61% of job seekers now reporting they have been ghosted after an interview.

This report documents the scale, causes, and consequences of this epidemic, establishing the empirical foundation for transparent accountability in hiring through the GhostScore platform.

Conclusion: The Case for Transparency

The data is unequivocal: employer ghosting is not a series of isolated incidents but a systemic failure affecting the majority of job seekers. The practice has accelerated dramatically, with a 112% increase since 2020, and shows no signs of improving without external intervention.

This epidemic damages not only candidates‚Äîcosting them time, money, and emotional wellbeing‚Äîbut also the companies themselves through brand erosion, pipeline destruction, and increased hiring costs.
